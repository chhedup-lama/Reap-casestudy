Market Opportunity & Business Case

Quantifying the revenue unlock, operational savings, and competitive advantage from AI-assisted KYB — and the cost of the status quo.

Revenue at Stake

KYB delays create two distinct revenue leaks — deferred activation and permanent abandonment. The model below sizes both across three growth scenarios.

Delayed Activation Revenue
Approved customers wait for card, payments & treasury access. Every day of avoidable KYB delay defers monthly account revenue from already-qualified accounts.
Lost Conversion (Abandonment)
Friction causes applicants to drop off before activation. Unlike deferral, abandonment permanently eliminates customer LTV — with no recovery path.

Competitive Landscape

Fast onboarding is becoming table stakes. Where peers set aggressive public SLAs, any gap between Reap's marketed speed and actual KYB throughput directly erodes conversion and trust.

Takeaway
Fast business onboarding is becoming table stakes. Reap's own 72-hour promise places direct pressure on the internal KYB process to perform consistently at that level. Any sustained gap between marketed speed and actual operational throughput increases conversion risk and weakens customer trust.

Investment vs. Return

The business case is a structured investment model — not a single efficiency estimate. Investments span build, run, adoption, and contingency. Returns span reviewer efficiency, compliance savings, revenue protection, and platform reuse. This structure lets leadership assess near-term payback and strategic value separately.
