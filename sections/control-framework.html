Block 5 — Trust, Reliability, and Governance

Control, Quality & Operating Framework

Defines who can act, what standards the system follows, what requires human review, and how policy-aligned decisions are enforced — making KYB not just functional, but controllable, explainable, and safe to operate.

Phase 1 Governance Detailed · 5 scope areas
Objective
Create the minimum governance structure required to operate AI-assisted KYB review in a compliant, auditable, and policy-aligned manner.
1
Policy-Aligned Decision Framework
Recommendations, follow-ups, and review outcomes must be grounded in approved policy — not free-form model behavior.
  • Approved policy sources for grounding with clear linkage to recommendations
  • Rule-aware review guidance and jurisdiction/segment-specific policy application
  • Policy version preserved at time of every decision
2
Human Decision Rights
Clear boundaries between what AI may assist with and what remains human-controlled — with no ambiguity around final authority.
Examples
  • AI may suggest missing info, risks, or follow-up language
  • AI may not independently approve or reject a business
  • Final decision authority stays with authorised reviewers
  • Escalations remain visible and reviewable at all times
3
Review and Escalation Rules
Defined conditions that pause normal progression and require manual review or escalation — predictable behavior under both normal and exception conditions.
  • Low-confidence extraction or conflicting evidence across documents
  • Missing critical fields or policy ambiguity
  • Potential sanctions, risk flags, or repeated processing failures
4
Change Control Foundations
Lightweight but explicit controls over changes to policies, prompts, workflow rules, and processing logic — even before full admin tooling arrives.
  • Version tracking with approval ownership
  • Release visibility and rollback readiness for material changes
5
Governance Operating Model
Clearly defined ownership across product, engineering, compliance, and operations — no ambiguity around who controls what.
CompliancePolicy interpretation and regulatory alignment
ProductWorkflow design and reviewer experience
EngineeringPlatform reliability and release safety
OperationsExecution health and exception handling
Phase 1 — Governance Outcome
Clear decision boundaries, policy-grounded behavior, defined human control points, and enough operational governance to launch responsibly in a live KYB setting.
Phase 1 Access Control Detailed · 5 scope areas
Objective
Implement role-based, tenant-safe access controls that protect case data, reviewer actions, and administrative capabilities — embedded from the start, not added later.
1
Role-Based Access Model
Permissions aligned to operational responsibility across five typical roles.
  • KYB Analyst · Senior Reviewer · Ops Manager · Compliance Lead · Platform Admin
  • Each role has defined permissions: case visibility, edit rights, review & escalation actions, admin, policy/configuration access
2
Tenant Isolation
All data access is tenant-safe — users access only cases, documents, and views belonging to their authorised tenant context.
  • Applies across: database patterns, search & retrieval results, file access, workflow queues, logs, and exports
3
Action-Level Permissions
Screen-level access is not sufficient — the platform controls who can perform sensitive operations.
  • Change case status, override recommendations, re-trigger processing
  • View sensitive evidence, close or reject a case
  • Modify operational rules or configurations
4
Service-to-Service Security
Background services, processing pipelines, and integrations operate with scoped service identities and least-privilege permissions — no over-permissioned service accounts.
5
Access Auditability
Material access and permission-sensitive actions are traceable — especially for high-risk cases, sensitive evidence, and admin operations.
Phase 1 — Access Control Outcome
Role-based access, tenant isolation, and action-level controls enforced strongly enough to support production use in a regulated workflow.

Audit
Audit Logs
Lightly touched
  • End-to-end event history per case
  • User action logging: review, edit, escalation, decision
  • Model output and recommendation logging
  • Evidence, policy version, and reviewer action linkage
  • Exportable audit views for internal/external review
Confidence
Confidence & Fallback Framework
Lightly touched
  • Confidence thresholds by extraction and recommendation class
  • Abstention behavior for low-confidence outputs
  • Fallback to manual review or escalation
  • Reviewer visibility into uncertainty signals
  • Policy-driven controls on when automation must stop
Evals
Evaluation / Evals Platform
Lightly touched
  • Golden datasets and scenario-based test suites
  • Offline evals: extraction, grounding, recommendation, follow-up
  • Regression testing across model or prompt changes
  • Scorecards by workflow step and error category
  • Release gating for material quality drops
Models
Model & Prompt Management
Lightly touched
  • Versioned prompts and model registry
  • Environment-safe rollout controls with rollback support
  • Prompt and model comparison workflows
  • Traceability of which version generated which output
Observability
Observability & Tracing
Lightly touched
  • Tracing across workflow, retrieval, model, and decision steps
  • Latency tracking by stage; failure categorisation
  • Queue health and retry visibility
  • Operational dashboards for support, engineering, and product
