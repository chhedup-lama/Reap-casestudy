Confidence Scoring & Uncertainty Handling

How the platform quantifies AI certainty, maps every score to a routing decision, and ensures no case reaches a reviewer with an inflated confidence signal.

Score every signal → Compose a weighted confidence → Map to routing band → Surface uncertainty to reviewer → Calibrate continuously
Score Composition
Weighted composite of 4 signal dimensions
Band Routing
5 score bands → queue assignment
Uncertainty Signals
6 explicit penalty conditions
Calibration
Threshold tuning from reviewer feedback data

1 Responsibilities & Scope

This Layer Does
  • Compose a unified confidence score from four sub-signals
  • Assign every AI output to a routing band (High → Policy Flag)
  • Detect and penalise explicit uncertainty conditions
  • Override band routing when policy flag conditions are met
  • Surface uncertainty reasons to reviewers in plain language
  • Log score breakdown for calibration and audit
Not in Scope
  • Does not produce the decision — only routes it
  • Does not override reviewer judgement
  • Does not synthesise evidence (handled by Layer 5.4)
  • Does not manage queue SLAs or capacity (Reviewer Assist layer)
  • Does not store historical reviewer decisions

2 Score Composition — Four Sub-Signals

The confidence score is a weighted average of four independently computed signals. Each signal is normalised to 0–1 before weighting.

35%
Extraction Completeness
How many required fields were successfully extracted vs expected per document type
30%
Retrieval Relevance
Average semantic similarity score across all retrieved chunks used in the synthesis
25%
Finding Clarity
Per-criterion clarity: whether the LLM found unambiguous, citation-backed evidence for each criterion
10%
LLM Self-Certainty
Model-reported confidence token probability aggregated across generated claims
0.35 × Extraction + 0.30 × Retrieval + 0.25 × Clarity + 0.10 × LLM Self-Cert. = Confidence Score [0–1]
Penalty override: Any active uncertainty signal (Section 4) applies a fixed penalty on top of the weighted score. If a policy flag rule triggers, routing is escalated to Compliance Lead regardless of the numeric score.

Very Low < 0.40 Escalate → Compliance Lead Human-only decision; AI output flagged as insufficient No — flagged insufficient

4 Uncertainty Signals & Penalties

When any signal below is detected, a fixed penalty is subtracted from the composite score before band assignment. Multiple signals stack.

Missing Required Documents
One or more mandatory document types absent for this entity/jurisdiction
−0.20 penalty
OCR Quality Below Threshold
Document confidence score from OCR engine falls below 0.75 minimum
−0.15 penalty
Jurisdiction Not in Policy Store
Entity's operating jurisdiction has no matching policy or regulation chunk
−0.20 penalty
Contradictory Evidence
Two or more documents surface conflicting facts for the same criterion
−0.25 penalty
Sanctions / Watchlist Match
Entity name or UBO matches a sanctioned entity name with ≥ 0.85 similarity
→ Policy Flag override
High-Risk Jurisdiction Flag
Entity incorporated or operating in a FATF grey-list / black-list country
→ Policy Flag override

5 Policy Flag Override Rules

Regardless of composite score, any of the following conditions immediately routes the case to the Compliance Lead queue and suppresses AI decision display.

1
Sanctions or watchlist name match (≥ 0.85 similarity)
2
Entity in FATF grey-list or black-list jurisdiction
3
UBO ownership ≥ 25% by a PEP (politically exposed person)
4
Shell company pattern detected (zero employees, nominee directors)
5
Adverse media hit above configurable relevance threshold
6
Manual compliance team escalation flag set on entity

6 Scoring Flow

1
Receive synthesis output from Layer 5.4
Inputs: per-criterion findings, citation map, retrieval relevance scores, extraction completeness report
2
Compute four sub-signals independently
Each signal normalised 0–1; logged separately for audit
extraction_completeness retrieval_relevance_avg finding_clarity llm_self_certainty
3
Apply weighted formula → raw composite score
35% Extraction + 30% Retrieval + 25% Clarity + 10% LLM Self-Cert
4
Detect uncertainty signals and apply penalties
Each active signal subtracts its fixed penalty; penalties stack; result floored at 0.0
5
Check policy flag override rules
If any flag rule triggers: skip band assignment, set routing = Compliance Lead
6
Assign final band and queue routing
Score mapped to High / Medium / Low / Very Low band; queue assignment written to case record
7
Write confidence payload to case record and emit to Reviewer Assist
Payload includes: composite score, sub-signal breakdown, active penalties, band, routing decision, uncertainty reasons for reviewer display

7 Calibration Strategy

Initial Thresholds (P1 Launch)
  • Conservative defaults: High band starts at 0.85 to minimise false positive auto-routing
  • All bands configurable via admin settings — no hardcoded values in runtime code
  • Sub-signal weights are environment variables, not model weights — no retraining required to tune
  • Penalty magnitudes set conservatively; loosened once acceptance rate data is available
Ongoing Calibration (P2+)
  • Track reviewer override rate per band — target < 15% override on High band
  • Track false positive rate: cases where High score was given but reviewer changed decision
  • Bi-weekly calibration review; threshold changes require Compliance Lead sign-off
  • Calibration history versioned and auditable — each threshold change logged with rationale

8 Reviewer-Facing Uncertainty Display

Principle: Reviewers are never shown a raw numeric score. They see a band label (e.g. Medium Confidence), the top uncertainty reasons in plain English, and a confidence indicator bar. Score arithmetic stays internal.
What is Shown to Reviewer
  • Confidence band label + colour indicator
  • Plain-language uncertainty reasons (e.g. "OCR quality low on Certificate of Incorporation")
  • Which criteria had low-confidence findings highlighted in the summary
  • Policy flag reasons if applicable (e.g. "Jurisdiction flagged: Iran")
What is Hidden from Reviewer
  • Raw composite score (avoids anchoring bias)
  • Sub-signal breakdown and individual weights
  • LLM token probability values
  • Penalty arithmetic and configuration parameters

9 Failure Modes & Safe Defaults

Failure Risks to Mitigate
  • Score inflation: High score on incomplete evidence due to poorly tuned weights
  • Anchoring effect: Reviewer over-trusts AI band and rubber-stamps decisions
  • Calibration drift: Thresholds not updated as model or retrieval quality changes
  • Penalty evasion: Edge cases that don't trigger signals despite poor evidence
Safe-Fail Defaults
  • Any scoring error routes to Very Low band, never silently passes
  • Missing sub-signal defaults to 0.0, not skipped in calculation
  • Score payload always logged regardless of routing outcome
  • Override rate alerts trigger at 20% threshold for immediate calibration review

11 Design Goals

No case is auto-routed to High band with incomplete or contradictory evidence
All scoring logic is auditable; every score can be reconstructed from the log
Thresholds are configuration, not code — tunable without deployment
Reviewers receive actionable uncertainty context, not raw numbers
Policy flag overrides are deterministic and never depend on score arithmetic
Calibration loop closes within two weeks of new reviewer feedback data
Scoring failures default to lowest band — never inflate confidence silently
Compliance oversight is mandatory for all sanctioned-entity and jurisdiction flags
