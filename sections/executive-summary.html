Block 1 — Executive Framing

Executive Summary

A single-page synthesis of the problem, solution, phased delivery approach, and investment ask.

The Problem

KYB today operates as a manual, fragmented, and error-prone bottleneck across onboarding. Analysts spend significant time reading unstructured documents, reconciling data across sources, and translating policies into checks—resulting in slow turnaround times, inconsistent decisions, and repeated back-and-forth with customers. This not only delays revenue activation and increases drop-off, but also introduces compliance risk through missed discrepancies and weak audit trails. As application volumes grow, the current model does not scale linearly with headcount and will become a capacity and control-breaking point for the business.

The Solution

We propose an AI-Assisted KYB Review Workspace (Review Copilot) that combines:

  • Document Intelligence — automated extraction, structuring, and summarization of KYB documents
  • Knowledge, Retrieval, and Reasoning — policy-grounded retrieval, evidence assembly, and discrepancy detection
  • Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Review — AI prepares the case, surfaces insights, and drafts actions; humans retain full decision control

The system follows an "AI prepares, human decides" model—ensuring speed without compromising compliance. The solution is built as a platform-first architecture, where core capabilities (document processing, retrieval, reasoning, review workflows, audit logging) are reusable across adjacent use cases such as KYC, AML investigations, and underwriting.

Investment Ask

The initiative requires staged investment across product, engineering, AI/ML, and compliance enablement:

  • Phase 1: Core squad (Product, Design, Frontend, Backend, AI/ML, Data) + initial infrastructure and model usage — TBD
  • Phase 2: Expansion into retrieval/reasoning systems, evaluation harness, and policy integration — TBD
  • Phase 3: Workflow orchestration, integrations, audit/compliance layers, and scaling infra — TBD
  • Phase 4: Incremental investment for reuse across additional workflows — TBD

Costs include headcount (product, engineering, AI/ML, data, compliance SME), infrastructure (compute, storage, orchestration), and model and vendor costs (OCR, LLM inference, external data sources).

The Ask

Approval to proceed with:

  • Phase 1 detailed scoping and solution design
  • Formation of a cross-functional squad
  • Validation of assumptions with Compliance, Ops, and GTM stakeholders
  • Establishment of baseline metrics and evaluation framework

This phase will de-risk the approach, validate ROI assumptions, and define the path to scaled rollout.
