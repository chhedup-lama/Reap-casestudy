Block 3 — Product Scope

Out of Scope & Constraints

What this product deliberately does not do — and the non-negotiable guardrails that shape everything it does.

The MVP is a human-in-the-loop review copilot — not an automated decision engine. AI prepares, retrieves, and recommends. Humans decide.
MVP Boundary vs Future Capability
Dimension MVP Boundary Future Capability
Decisioning Human makes every final decision →Selective automation for low-risk, high-confidence scenarios
AI Role Extract, summarize, retrieve, recommend →Recommend + orchestrate bounded actions
Workflow Basic case states and review actions →Advanced routing, prioritization, and cross-team orchestration
Documents Core KYB documents only →Wider document coverage and more flexible ingestion
Policy Use Retrieved guidance for reviewer support →Structured policy reasoning and rule-driven enforcement
Data Sources Primarily uploaded / internal documents →External verification and enrichment sources
Quality Low-confidence cases routed to human review →More granular confidence-aware automation
Learning Loop Reviewer corrections captured →Governed improvement loop and eval-driven model upgrades
Reporting Essential audit and ops visibility →Rich analytics, productivity insights, and portfolio trends
Scope Principles
Reviewer usabilityoverworkflow breadth
Auditabilityoverautomation depth
Extraction qualityovermodel complexity
Operational safetyoverstraight-through processing

Hard Constraints

Non-negotiable guardrails that shape the solution. Cannot be traded away for speed, scope, or convenience.

Regulatory
Final approval and rejection decisions must remain with an authorized human reviewer in Phase 1
→System can recommend, draft, and prioritize — but cannot autonomously approve or reject
Every recommendation must be evidence-backed and traceable to source documents and applicable policy
→Outputs must include citations, linked evidence, and policy grounding
Decision history must be audit-reconstructable at any point in time
→Reviewer actions, model outputs, edits, overrides, timestamps, and policy versions must be logged
Policy interpretation authority remains with Compliance / Legal
→The product cannot invent or reinterpret policy without human ownership
Technical
Must operate on top of existing case and document systems — not require a full platform replacement
→Architecture integrates with current workflow, storage, and verification systems
Uploaded documents and case data may be incomplete, low-quality, or inconsistent
→Must support abstention, missing-data handling, and confidence-based fallback
Retrieval must be scoped to the correct policy set, case context, and jurisdiction
→Strong metadata, access controls, and retrieval logic are required
PII and sensitive business data must be handled within enterprise security controls
→Data access, storage, logging, and model usage must meet security and privacy requirements
External verification data may depend on third-party systems with latency or availability limits
→Workflow must handle connector failure, timeout, and degraded-mode review
Organisational
Analysts and compliance teams will not adopt a tool that increases review time or documentation burden
→UX must reduce swivel-chair work and fit into existing review habits
Compliance, Risk, and Ops must be able to trust and explain system behavior
→The copilot must be transparent, editable, and controllable — not a black box
Engineering capacity is finite — Phase 1 must be implementable without building a large custom AI platform
→Prioritize high-value workflow assist over broad platform ambition
Change management in regulated operations is slow and review-heavy
→Rollout should support phased adoption, shadow mode, controlled pilots, and measurable guardrails
Operational
SLA performance cannot materially degrade during rollout
→Introduction of the copilot must not slow queues, even during pilot stages
Escalation paths and accountability must remain clear at all times
→The system must never obscure who made the final judgment or why
