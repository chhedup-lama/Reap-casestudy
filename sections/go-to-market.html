Block 8 — Delivery Strategy

Go-to-Market & Rollout

De-risk compliance exposure, build analyst trust, and prove measurable operational value before broad release — controlled stages, clear success gates, structured enablement, and explicit change management.

1 Rollout Principles

Non-negotiable design rules for this launch
Launch in controlled stages — never big-bang
Validate AI quality before analyst exposure
Human judgement stays fully in control until trust is earned
Hard go/no-go thresholds for each stage — not date-driven
Measure operational impact and compliance safety in parallel
Treat rollout as a product launch and an operating-model change

2 Rollout Stages

Alpha Internal / Shadow
Scope 2–3 analysts · AI runs in parallel on live cases in shadow mode
Duration 2–4 weeks
Exit threshold
Suggestion quality validated · No critical grounding or policy failures
Beta Controlled Rollout
Scope Full KYB analyst team · AI outputs visible but non-binding
Duration 4–6 weeks
Exit threshold
Acceptance rate ≥ TBD · Override patterns understood · No compliance incidents
GA Full Rollout
Scope All incoming eligible cases routed through AI-assisted workspace
Duration 2–4 weeks post-Beta decision
Exit threshold
SLA improvement confirmed · Stable quality metrics · Operational readiness signed off

3 Shadow Mode Design

During Alpha, AI runs on the same live cases as analysts but outputs don't influence active decisions. Human and AI outputs are compared offline to validate quality before any analyst-facing exposure.

Field extraction accuracy
Summary quality & missing-info detection
Recommendation usefulness
Citation grounding quality
False confidence & abstention behavior
Policy alignment across representative case types
Shadow mode should also surface where the system performs poorly, which case types should be excluded initially, and what safeguards are required before Beta proceeds.

4 Pilot Design

Recommended Pilot Design
  • Start with a limited analyst cohort
  • Representative mix of low- and medium-complexity cases
  • Exclude highest-risk or highly ambiguous edge cases initially
  • Compare analyst-only vs AI-assisted handling patterns
  • Track both quality and throughput — not speed alone
  • Weekly review checkpoints across Product, Ops, Compliance, Engineering
Key Pilot Decisions
  • Which case types are in or out of scope
  • What counts as a critical failure
  • When analyst-visible suggestions can be enabled
  • What triggers rollback, pause, or narrower rollout

7 Analyst Training Programme

Analysts are trained on
  • What the AI workspace does and does not do
  • How to interpret summaries, extracted fields, and recommendations
  • How to verify citation-backed evidence
  • How to read confidence and uncertainty indicators
  • When to override, escalate, or ignore suggestions
  • How to annotate corrections and capture useful feedback
  • What remains the analyst's responsibility at decision time
Recommended Format
1Short role-based onboarding session
2Guided walkthrough of real case examples
3Sandbox or supervised practice cases
4Decision playbooks for common scenarios
5Refresher sessions after Beta findings

8 Change Management

Key Messages to Analysts
AI supports judgement — it does not replace reviewer accountability
Final decision authority stays with human reviewers
Overrides are expected and valuable — not a failure signal
Feedback improves the system — it is not performance management
The goal is to remove repetitive work and improve consistency — not automate away expertise
Supporting Actions
Identify analyst champions early
Create a structured feedback loop during rollout
Publish known limitations transparently
Review override themes without blame
Communicate go-live criteria clearly so trust is built through discipline

9 Operational Readiness

Confirm readiness across product, compliance, operations, and engineering before each stage.

Agreed success and failure thresholds defined
Support ownership confirmed during rollout
Incident escalation path established
Rollback / feature-disable mechanism tested
Analyst training completed
Quality and operational health monitoring in place
In-scope and out-of-scope case definitions agreed

10 Exit Criteria by Stage

Alpha exits when
Offline quality is validated — extraction accuracy, grounding, and policy alignment confirmed on representative case types. No critical failures observed.
Beta exits when
Live analyst usage is stable and compliance-safe. Override patterns are understood. Acceptance rate meets threshold. No compliance incidents recorded.
GA proceeds when
Measurable SLA or productivity gains are confirmed. Control effectiveness is not degraded. Operational and compliance readiness signed off across all functions.

11 Non-Goals for Initial Rollout

Fully autonomous case decisions
Universal support for all edge-case KYB scenarios from day one
Unrestricted use across all geographies or risk tiers
Automatic learning from live feedback without governed review
Replacing existing compliance controls
Rollout Principle
Rollout success depends as much on trust, training, and operational discipline as on model quality. The launch should be gated not only by AI performance, but also by analyst adoption, compliance safety, and operational readiness.
