Block 3 — Product Scope

Working Assumptions

Practical working assumptions that make the roadmap and design concrete. Directionally realistic, not operational facts — intended to be validated and refined as discovery progresses.

How to use these
Each assumption informs a scope, phasing, or build-vs-buy decision. Where an assumption is wrong, adjust scope or phasing accordingly. Grouped into three themes: Operational Reality (volume, effort, policy), Technical Environment (systems, data, storage), and Design & Strategy (HITL, stakeholders, platform vision).
Operational Reality Volume  ·  Effort  ·  Policy
1
Case Volume & Throughput
  • Recurring monthly volume is high enough that per-case time savings create clear operational value
  • Volume is uneven — queue build-ups track sales and onboarding cycles
  • Mix of low / medium / escalated-complexity cases exists across the queue
  • A small cohort of complex cases consumes a disproportionate share of Ops & Compliance time
  • Goal is to improve both average handling time and queue predictability — not just peak throughput
2
Manual Effort & Workflow
  • ~40 min average per case is directionally correct, spanning: document review, entity assessment, SOP lookup, follow-up drafting, and re-review
  • Effort is not evenly distributed — document classification, fact reconstruction, and follow-up management dominate
  • Re-review loops are the biggest source of avoidable effort
  • Rule-based WF assignment already exists for KYB case ownership don't rebuild
  • Distinct queue loads exist across KYB Analysts, Ops Reviewers, Compliance Reviewers, and Team Leads
3
Policy & Rules Environment
  • Eligibility rules, risk appetite guidance, and SOPs already exist and are stable enough for retrieval-based workflows
  • Policy changes occasionally but won't immediately obsolete a structured policy layer
  • Core challenge is translating narrative policy into consistent operational decisions — not lack of policy itself
  • Grey-area cases requiring judgment and escalation are expected and appropriate
  • An internal CMS for documentation exists (CRUD capable); policy owners are available to tag and interpret content during build
Technical Environment Systems  ·  Data  ·  Storage
4
Internal Tools & Systems
  • An internal case-review / admin tool exists for final decisioning and account activation
  • Accessible inputs include: form data, uploaded docs, analyst notes, follow-up comms, and policy content
  • Tooling can be extended via APIs, workflow integrations, or embedded UI components
  • External vendor integration is possible for OCR, screening data, and model access where appropriate
  • Sufficient engineering flexibility exists to layer AI assistance above the core ops system without rebuilding it
5
Data Quality & Variability
  • Submitted documents vary in quality, structure, and completeness
  • Inputs include scanned PDFs, images, screenshots, and mixed formats — extraction will be imperfect
  • Form fields, uploaded documents, and registry data won't always align cleanly
  • Platform must be designed around "assist and verify" — not extract once and trust blindly
  • Sufficient historical case data exists for initial evaluation and workflow design, even if not fully labelled
6
Data Ingestion & Tenancy
  • KYB data enters via multiple channels: forms, uploads, resubmissions, analyst files, and external providers
  • Source documents remain in secure Reap-controlled storage
  • Must Build Ingestion → parsing → extraction → indexing pipelines layered above existing systems of record
  • Raw artifacts, case metadata, retrieval-ready content, and audit logs stored in separate but linked layers
  • Platform is tenant-safe and permission-aware by design: case-level access controls, encrypted storage, scoped retrieval, and full auditability
Design & Strategy HITL  ·  Stakeholders  ·  Platform Vision
7
Human-in-the-Loop Decisioning
  • Final onboarding decisions remain human-owned throughout Phase 1
  • Initial objective is assistive decision support — not autonomous approval or rejection
  • AI outputs must be reviewable, editable, and grounded in inspectable evidence
  • Reviewers can always: inspect source evidence, accept/edit/reject suggestions, override recommendations, and document rationale
  • Architecture must support staged evolution: HITL → selective autonomy — controlled via admin-managed policy and risk thresholds, without rebuilding the system
8
Stakeholders & Operating Model
  • Primary users: KYB Analysts and Operations Reviewers
  • Key cross-functional: Compliance Policy (define logic & escalation rules), Compliance/Risk (handle escalations)
  • Secondary stakeholders: Sales/Onboarding (onboarding speed), Audit/Legal/Security (traceability & access controls)
  • Successful rollout requires tight weekly collaboration across Product, Engineering, Ops, and Compliance — not a handoff model
9
Success & Platform Scope
  • Success is measured by quality and control — not speed alone
  • First 6 months: improve efficiency, review consistency, follow-up quality, auditability, and user trust/adoption
  • Business prefers a phased, low-risk rollout with measurable gains over an aggressive push to end-to-end automation
  • KYB is the lighthouse use case — platform reuse targeted for: EDD, sanctions/adverse media, support ops, payment exceptions
  • Commodity infrastructure: buy or integrate. Differentiated workflow intelligence, policy reasoning, review UX, and eval controls: build in-house
