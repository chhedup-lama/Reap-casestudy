1What This Layer Does

Responsibilities
  • Store retrieval-ready chunks and metadata
  • Generate embeddings and vector representations
  • Retrieve relevant case evidence
  • Retrieve applicable policy clauses
  • Assemble evidence and policy into bounded context
  • Attach citations to findings
  • Support gap detection, drafting, and recommendations
Not in Scope Here
  • Final human decisions
  • Sending applicant communications autonomously
  • Resolving material ambiguity without reviewer oversight
Those come later in reviewer assist and workflow control.

3Core Modules

Knowledge & Retrieval
Does
  • Indexes chunks, entities, and case-level evidence
  • Stores embeddings and retrieval metadata
  • Performs semantic and filtered retrieval
  • Supports case-scoped evidence lookup
Engine that finds the right evidence instead of relying on model memory.
Policy Retrieval
Does
  • Indexes policies as governed retrievable units
  • Filters policies by applicability
  • Selects the relevant rules for the case
  • Preserves hierarchy, version, and effective dates
Must retrieve the right policy for the right jurisdiction, entity type, and review context — not generically.
Citation & Evidence
Does
  • Binds findings to source evidence
  • Distinguishes case citations from policy citations
  • Supports reviewer evidence inspection
  • Preserves provenance for every claim
Makes every output traceable, reviewable, and audit-safe.
Rules & Decisioning
Does
  • Applies deterministic applicability logic
  • Supports policy family selection
  • Converts evidence + policy into bounded review outputs
  • Surfaces missing evidence, conflict, or escalation conditions
Keeps retrieval policy-aware and controlled — not open-ended search.

6Retrieval & Policy Grounding Design

This layer answers four questions for every case:

Q1
What evidence do we have?
Q2
Which policies apply?
Q3
What supports or conflicts?
Q4
What context passes forward?
Separation matters: Case evidence (what exists) and policy (what is required) must be treated as distinct corpora — not one undifferentiated search space.

8Policy Retrieval & Selection Logic

Policy Indexing Preserves
  • Policy hierarchy + clause-level retrieval units
  • Active vs draft / retired status
  • Effective dates + jurisdiction
  • Entity type + product / onboarding context
  • Risk tier + review stage
  • Escalation flags
Selection: Two Steps
  • Step 1: Deterministic applicability logic
  • Step 2: Retrieval-based ranking within the applicable policy family
Prefer
Active over inactive Specific over generic Mandatory over guidance Jurisdiction-specific over global Latest effective version
Typical Policy Families
Onboarding policy Prohibited / restricted business Ownership verification Address validation Escalation / exception High-risk jurisdiction

9Citation & Evidence Design

Citation Requirements
  • Each criterion finding includes a citation where evidence exists
  • Recommendations link back to all underlying criterion citations
  • Policy citation = the rule applied
  • Case citation = the evidence used
Citation Resolves To
  • Document name
  • Page / section where possible
  • Chunk ID for auditability
  • Snippet preview in reviewer UI
Guardrail: No claim is shown as factual unless backed by retrieved evidence. If citation binding fails, the output is marked incomplete or the unsupported claim is withheld.

10Valid Evidence Rules

Valid Evidence — Must Be
  • From current case or approved policy corpus
  • Retrievable and inspectable
  • Linked to specific source location
  • Relevant to the criterion
  • Sufficiently legible / extractable
  • Not superseded where version matters
  • Tenant-safe and case-safe
  • Not inferred only from model memory
Weak / Invalid Evidence Examples
  • Unsupported model inference
  • Vague summary without source
  • Very low-confidence OCR text
  • Stale policy version
  • Retrieval without provenance
  • Unrelated document snippet
  • Context-stripped fragment

12Weak / No Evidence Handling

Weak Evidence Examples
  • Partially relevant citation
  • Low-quality OCR or low-confidence extraction
  • Evidence supports only part of the claim
  • Conflicting sources
No Evidence Examples
  • Required document absent
  • Retrieval returns nothing relevant
  • Citation binding fails
  • No applicable policy found
Required behavior: Lower confidence · Soften recommendation · State what is missing · Propose follow-up or escalation · Abstain where thresholds require · Prevent unsupported downstream decision support.

13Retrieval Failure & Safe-Fail

Key Failure Risks
  • Wrong policy retrieved
  • Relevant policy omitted
  • Old document version retrieved
  • Weak chunking hurts retrieval
  • Semantic similarity returns irrelevant content
  • Extraction errors propagate into grounding
  • Too much raw text overloads context
  • Conflicting evidence not surfaced
  • Provenance missing
  • Policy index is stale
Safe-Fail Behavior
  • Avoid strong approve/reject language
  • Mark criterion as insufficiently grounded
  • Surface missing policy / missing evidence warning
  • Show confidence / coverage gaps
  • Route to reviewer or specialist handling
  • Log failure for monitoring and evals
It is better to return insufficient grounding than a polished but unsafe output.
