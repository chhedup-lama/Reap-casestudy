Block 4 — End to End Platform Components enabling KYB

Reviewer Assist / Case Intelligence

Converts grounded case evidence and policy context into a reviewer-facing intelligence surface — summaries, structured findings, gap detection, draft support, and governed decision controls.

Grounded evidence in → Orient reviewer → Summarise case → Surface gaps & open items → Assist next action → Human decides
Case Summary
Grounded overview of entity, findings & status
Checklisting
Policy & case-state requirements as review checks
Gap Detection
Missing docs, fields, conflicts & open items
Action Assist
Draft support, delta analysis & next-best actions

1 What This Layer Does

This Layer Does
  • Summarise the case in reviewer-friendly form
  • Present extracted fields and evidence clearly
  • Highlight missing, low-confidence, or conflicting items
  • Generate actionable checklists and open items
  • Support draft notes and follow-up requests
  • Suggest next-best actions with visible uncertainty
  • Preserve human judgment and explicit decision control
Not in Scope
  • Does not replace the system of record
  • Does not make final autonomous decisions
  • Does not hide uncertainty or unsupported reasoning
  • Does not bypass role-based workflow controls
  • Does not replace the enterprise CRM / case management stack

2 Product Delivery Model

This layer is a review experience layer, not a net-new end-to-end case system. Three practical delivery options:

1
Embedded Workspace
AI-assisted panels added inside existing ops tooling. Reviewers stay in familiar systems.
Best MVP assumption
2
Lightweight Dedicated Console
Standalone review UI when existing systems are too rigid for the required UX.
Fallback delivery
3
Headless Platform + Reference Workspace
Core product stays as APIs and embeddable components; UI is a thin optional consumption layer.
Advanced / API-first
Recommended PRD Stance Platform-first · UI-light · Human-in-the-loop · Existing-system compatible

3

Extracted fields, original documents, and evidence links displayed side by side.

Missing documents, missing fields, low-confidence outputs, conflicts, policy blockers, and follow-up needs — surfaced explicitly.

Checklisting, follow-up drafting, delta analysis on re-review, recommendation support.

4 Core Modules

KYB Case Summary
What it does
  • Concise case overview with known facts and major issues
  • Surfaces missing items and unresolved checks
  • Links key statements back to underlying evidence
Helps reviewer understand the case in seconds without replacing evidence inspection.
Checklisting
What it does
  • Converts policy and case-state requirements into visible review checks
  • Shows satisfied, unresolved, and pending items
  • Supports reviewer modification or override
Turns grounded intelligence into actionable operational review state.
Gap Detection / Open Items
What it does
  • Highlights missing docs, fields, inconsistencies, and unresolved issues
  • Groups issues by category; preserves history across review cycles
  • Supports system-generated and reviewer-created open items
Helps reviewer know exactly what is still blocking progress.
Drafting
What it does
  • Internal notes, follow-up request drafting, escalation summaries
  • Distinguishes AI-generated draft text from reviewer-authored text
  • Full reviewer editing before any content is sent or committed
Reduces effort while keeping outbound content editable and controlled.
Delta Analysis
What it does
  • Compares current review state with prior submissions or review cycles
  • Highlights what changed; shows what remains unresolved
  • Essential for re-review and follow-up handling
Prevents reviewers manually reconstructing what changed between iterations.
Action Recommendation
What it does
  • Suggests next-best actions (proceed / request follow-up / hold / escalate)
  • Uses grounded evidence and policy context
  • Presented as assistive, not authoritative
Supports speed and consistency on routine cases without replacing human accountability.

5 Reviewer Workspace — Design Goals

The workspace is the single primary operating surface for AI-assisted KYB review. It optimises for:

Speed on routine cases
Clarity on ambiguous cases
Traceability on material decisions
Low context switching
Inspectable AI support
Strong human control
Embeddable in existing ops tooling
Auditability of all AI vs human actions

6 Core Panels & Controls

Case Header
What it shows
  • Case ID, legal entity name, workflow status, assigned queue
  • Key timestamps, risk tier, review type
  • Intake source where relevant
Reviewer should understand operational context within seconds.
Summary Panel
What it shows
  • Concise AI-assisted case summary — facts distinct from interpretation
  • Submitted documents, major issues, missing items
  • Refreshes when material case inputs change
Accelerates orientation — does not replace evidence review.
Extracted Fields Panel
What it shows
  • Fields grouped by section: entity, registration, address, ownership
  • Source evidence link per field; editable by authorised reviewers
  • Missing, low-confidence, and conflicting fields marked
Reviewer edits captured as explicit overrides — not silent replacements.
Evidence Panel
What it shows
  • Original documents with navigation from finding to source
  • Side-by-side evidence and extracted field review
  • Policy evidence vs document evidence clearly distinguished
Essential for trust, speed, and auditability.
Checklist / Open Items Panel
What it shows
  • Missing docs, fields, incomplete submissions, unresolved checks
  • Items grouped by category with links to evidence or policy
  • Reviewer can resolve, reopen, or add manual items
Reviewer knows exactly what remains before a decision can be made.
Drafting Panel
What it supports
  • Internal notes, follow-up request drafts, escalation summaries
  • AI-generated vs reviewer-authored content clearly labelled
  • Templates for common requests; save without final decision
MVP: structured notes and follow-up draft support only.
Decision Controls
Actions available
  • Approve, Reject, Request Follow-up — explicit user action required
  • Warnings shown for unresolved critical items
  • Outcome, actor, timestamp, and rationale persisted
Final case disposition remains a human action — always.
Escalation Controls
Actions available
  • Escalate to senior reviewer, specialist queue, or compliance team
  • Requires escalation reason; carries forward case context
  • Preserves escalation history; supports return-from-escalation
Structured escalation — not ad hoc.

7 Copilot Behaviour & Interaction Model

Design principle: The copilot is an embedded reviewer-assist layer — not a freeform chatbot. It should reduce reviewer effort, not replace accountable reviewer judgment.
Copilot Does
  • Parse and surface structured information
  • Summarise the case
  • Highlight missing and conflicting evidence
  • Retrieve relevant policy content
  • Link all outputs to citations
  • Suggest next-best actions
  • Support drafting of notes, follow-ups, and escalation summaries
Copilot Does Not
  • Approve or reject cases autonomously in MVP
  • Present suggestions as final decisions
  • Invent unsupported facts
  • Hide uncertainty or low-confidence signals
  • Take irreversible workflow actions without reviewer confirmation
  • Bypass enterprise permissions or governance

8 Confidence, Citations & Editability

Three controls must be visible in every AI-assisted workspace interaction.

Confidence Display
  • Show indicators where available; avoid false precision
  • Distinguish high, low, and unavailable confidence
  • Allow reviewers to filter to low-confidence items
  • Explain confidence in reviewer-friendly language
Citation Behaviour
  • Link material factual outputs to document evidence
  • Link policy-grounded suggestions to policy source
  • Distinguish document, policy, and reviewer-note citations
  • Evidence easy to inspect — not buried
Editable Outputs
  • Allow authorised field edits and draft text editing
  • AI-generated and reviewer-authored content kept distinguishable
  • Preserve original system output when reviewer edits matter
  • Version and audit all material edits

11 Gap Detection & Open Item Logic

Design principle: The copilot should not only assess what is present — it must systematically detect what is missing, incomplete, inconsistent, or unresolved, then convert those gaps into actionable review items. This is where the platform provides real workflow value.

Six Gap Classes Detected

Missing Document
Required document absent
e.g. Certificate of incorporation, ownership declaration, director ID, proof of address
Missing Field
Document present but data absent
e.g. Registration number missing, address blank, issue date illegible, shareholding % absent
Inconsistency
Two or more sources conflict materially
e.g. Name mismatch, address differs across docs, UBO % don't reconcile, registration date conflicts
Sufficiency
Evidence exists but too weak to conclude
e.g. Partial ownership proof, outdated address, low-quality scan, unsigned declaration
Policy Coverage
Case outside supported policy logic
e.g. Jurisdiction not in policy store, entity type without a matching rule set
External Verification
Cannot resolve without external result
e.g. Internal doc claim needs registry or adverse media verification (when integrations exist)
Important nuance — missing document detection should distinguish: not uploaded · uploaded but unsupported format · uploaded but failed ingestion · uploaded but wrongly classified · uploaded but insufficient. This improves reviewer clarity and downstream ops handling.

Open Item — Required Fields

Open Item ID
Unique reference for tracking
Gap Type
Missing doc / field / inconsistency / etc.
Severity / Priority
Critical → Informational (see tiers below)
Trigger Source
System rule or reviewer-created
Plain-language Description
Reviewer-readable explanation
Linked Criterion
KYB criterion this gap affects
Evidence Reference
Supporting or missing evidence link
Recommended Action
e.g. Request document, confirm field, escalate
Status + Owner
Current lifecycle state & assigned party

AI-Proposed vs Reviewer-Confirmed Open Items

Auto-Generated (AI Proposes)
  • System produces gap items from detection logic
  • Surfaced to reviewer as suggestions, not confirmed items
  • Labelled as AI-generated throughout lifecycle
Reviewer-Confirmed (Human Confirms)
  • Reviewer accepts, edits, merges, rejects, or creates manually
  • Becomes the official workflow action item
  • Wording edits and confirmation tracked in audit log
Strong design pattern: AI proposes → Human confirms → System tracks both.

Open Item Lifecycle

Drafted by system→ Reviewed by analyst→ Sent to applicant / ops→ Awaiting response→ Response received→ Re-opened→ Resolved· Closed — not required· Superseded / merged

Gap Priority Tiers

Critical
Blocks decision until resolved — must be cleared before any final action
Major
Likely requires follow-up — decision possible but reviewer must annotate rationale
Minor
Reviewer should inspect — may not block decision; can be overridden with note
Informational
Surfaced for awareness only — no action required to proceed

12 Design Goals

Reduce reviewer orientation time on every case
Make grounded evidence easy to inspect and verify
Convert intelligence into actionable review tasks
Preserve reviewer control over edits, escalation, and final decisions
Distinguish system suggestions from human-authored outcomes
Surface uncertainty and blockers clearly — never hide them
Support embedding into existing operational tools
Improve productivity without weakening governance
