Workflow Orchestration & Human Review

Governs how cases move through review, when human intervention is required, and how AI-assisted outputs are safely converted into governed operational actions.

Case state changes → Route to review or intervention → Human control points → Override / escalate if needed → Human decides → Full audit trail
Workflow State
Transitions from intake to final disposition
Low-Confidence & Abstention
Safe degradation, not hidden uncertainty
Override & Escalation
First-class events, fully captured and auditable
Safe Automation Boundaries
AI prepares — humans stay accountable

1 Phase 1 Scope

The objective is to ensure the platform can move cases forward safely, not just generate intelligent outputs. Phase 1 covers:

Workflow state transitions Review-ready vs manual-intervention routing Explicit human control points Low-confidence & abstention handling Manual review gates Override capture Escalation routing Safe automation boundaries Audit logging of all workflow & reviewer actions

3 Human-in-the-Loop Design

Design principle: The KYB copilot is a decision-support system, not an autonomous compliance decision-maker. AI assists the workflow; humans remain accountable for the outcome.

Reviewers remain accountable for evidence validation, open item confirmation, follow-up handling, escalation, and final case disposition. Required human control points:

1
Review of AI-generated findings
2
Validation of evidence sufficiency
3
Confirmation or editing of open items
4
Override of incorrect system outputs
5
Approval of follow-up communication
6
Acceptance or rejection of recommendations
7
Escalation to specialist or compliance review
8
Final decision submission

4 Low-Confidence & Abstention Handling

Principle: Low confidence should trigger safe degradation — not hidden uncertainty. "Insufficient evidence to recommend" is safer than an unsupported conclusion.

Low-Confidence Triggers

Weak retrieval
Poor OCR quality
Conflicting evidence
Low extraction confidence
Unsupported policy mapping
Ambiguous entity resolution
Incomplete document coverage
Citation binding fails
Expected System Behaviour — Low Confidence
  • Reduce recommendation strength
  • Surface uncertainty explicitly to reviewer
  • Highlight likely causes in plain language
  • Direct reviewer attention to weak criteria
  • Suggest follow-up instead of forcing conclusions
  • Route to manual review where required
Abstention Conditions
  • Evidence is insufficient for any safe conclusion
  • Evidence conflicts materially across sources
  • Policy retrieval or citation binding fails
  • Ingestion quality is too poor to rely on
  • Case falls outside supported policy scope
  • Required document coverage is incomplete

Abstention Response Must Return

Reason for abstention Impacted criteria Missing evidence summary Recommended next action

5 Manual Review Gates

These gates must be policy-configurable — not embedded only in model behaviour. Certain case types always require manual review regardless of confidence score.

Poor-quality key evidence
Sanctions / adverse media flags
Policy exception scenarios
Unsupported document types or languages
Judgment-heavy escalations

6 Override & Escalation

Reviewer overrides are first-class system events — not exceptions to be silently absorbed. Every override must be captured in full.

What the system suggested

Timestamp
When the override occurred
Reviewer Identity / Role
Who overrode and at what authority level
Outcome Impact
Whether override changed the final decision

Escalation — What Must Be Carried Forward

Escalation reason Impacted criteria Evidence bundle Prior reviewer notes Open items Current status Assigned owner / queue
This ensures the next reviewer does not need to reconstruct the case manually.

7 Safe Automation Boundaries

Principle: Automate preparation, not accountability.
Suitable for AI Assistance
  • Summarising case evidence
  • Surfacing relevant policy
  • Highlighting missing documents
  • Proposing open items
  • Identifying inconsistencies
  • Drafting reviewer notes
  • Suggesting recommendation options
  • Preparing follow-up requests
Not Suitable for Autonomous Action
  • Final compliance approval or rejection
  • Policy exception granting
  • Irreversible disposition actions
  • Uncited factual assertions
  • Applicant-facing requests without human approval
  • Automatic resolution of material contradictions
  • Unsupported policy interpretation

8 Reviewer Trust & Experience

The workflow should help every reviewer answer three questions quickly:

Question 1
What did the system find?
Question 2
Why does it believe that?
Question 3
What should happen next?
To Support This, the Experience Must
  • Expose evidence before persuasion
  • Make uncertainty visible
  • Separate facts from recommendations
  • Distinguish system suggestions from approved decisions
  • Preserve manual control at all stages
  • Avoid black-box interactions

9 Auditability & Logging

Workflow, fallback, and reviewer actions must be fully logged to support decision reconstruction, QA, compliance audit, and model improvement.

11 Key Design Principles

Original document remains the source artifact
Processing outputs are versioned, not silently replaced
Low-quality inputs block or warn — never silently pass
Workflow state reflects real evidence and review readiness
Every reviewer action must be traceable
Human fallback must preserve auditability
Failure must be explicit and recoverable
Final case disposition remains a human action

12 Non-Goals

Replace reviewer judgment
Act on uncited reasoning
Silently resolve material contradictions
Auto-close evidence gaps without reviewer confirmation
Make final compliance decisions autonomously
Conceal uncertainty in polished language
Phase 1 Design Principle
Workflow orchestration should ensure AI-assisted review remains operationally usable, governed, and safe.
Help reviewers move cases forward with more speed and consistency — while preserving clear control over exceptions, escalations, and final decisions.
