Block 3 — Product Scope

Dependencies & Integration Points

Upstream systems, external services, and cross-functional approvals that directly affect Phase 1 readiness. Delivery risk made explicit early — especially where the KYB experience relies on systems or decisions outside the product team's control.

Dependency Principles

  • Prioritise dependencies that can block Phase 1 launch
  • Distinguish launch-critical from enhancement dependencies
  • Define clear owners for every dependency
  • Validate API and data contracts early — not during UAT
  • Design fallback paths where third-party or upstream systems are unreliable
  • Treat organisational approvals as real delivery dependencies, not side notes

Integration Expectations

For all launch-relevant integrations, validate before build completes:

  • Request and response schema
  • Authentication method
  • Rate limits and throughput constraints
  • Latency expectations
  • Retry and timeout behavior
  • Versioning and backward compatibility
  • Error codes and fallback handling
  • Audit/logging requirements
  • Test environment availability
  • Owner and escalation path

Organisational Dependencies

Cross-functional alignment and approvals that can delay launch if not resolved early:

  • Compliance approval on policy grounding and reviewer-control boundaries
  • Legal sign-off on acceptable AI usage and generated content boundaries
  • Security review for document handling, tenant isolation, and model/provider data flow
  • Risk approval for confidence thresholds, fallback rules, and escalation handling
  • Operations alignment on workflow ownership, queue design, and SLA expectations
  • Analyst manager buy-in for pilot design, shadow mode, and training participation
  • Data / AI governance review for model usage, prompt controls, and output retention
  • Engineering capacity alignment across platform, integrations, and product squads

Risks to Watch

  • Upstream systems expose incomplete or unstable data contracts
  • External providers have latency or reliability spikes during live review hours
  • Compliance policy content is not structured enough for retrieval and grounding
  • Multiple teams assume different ownership of workflow transitions
  • Sandbox/test environments do not reflect production behavior
  • Legal/security review arrives late and forces architectural rework
Phase 1 delivery risk is not model quality alone. It is dependency readiness across workflow, policy, extraction, security, and cross-functional approvals. These should be managed as first-class launch gates.
