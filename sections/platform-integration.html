Block 6 — Platform and Integration Layer

Platform & Integration Layer

The foundational backbone that makes every KYB capability dependable — case coordination, artifact storage, access control, workflow orchestration, audit, policy readiness, and operational instrumentation, phased across three delivery stages.

Phase 1
Platform Infrastructure
Case backbone · Artifact storage · IAM · Workflow orchestration · Job execution · Audit foundation · Policy layer · Observability · Extensibility
Phase 2
Communications & Learning

Analytics & Expansion

Phase 1 Platform Infrastructure Foundation · 9 capability areas
Objective
Build the minimum production-grade platform infrastructure needed to support end-to-end KYB operations with strong controls for security, traceability, workflow state management, and extensibility.
1
Case and Workflow Backbone
A canonical case object that acts as the system of coordination for the entire KYB lifecycle — every document, output, transition, and decision resolves to a single case context.
  • Unique case creation, lifecycle tracking, and metadata ownership
  • Workflow state transitions with status history and timestamps
  • Linkage across case, entity, documents, extracted outputs, review tasks, and decisions
  • Reprocessing and re-review without breaking traceability
2
Document and Artifact Storage
Secure storage of all source and derived artifacts. Originals remain immutable; processed outputs are versioned derivatives — not silent replacements.
  • Immutable original file retention with tenant-scoped access
  • Versioned processed artifacts: OCR text, extractions, summaries, chunks
  • Every derivative artifact linked to its source file with provenance and processing version
  • Multi-document-type support per case with retention controls
3
Identity, Access, and Tenant Isolation
Strict role-based access and tenant separation from day one. Security boundaries are not an enhancement — they are a launch requirement for KYB data.
  • Role-based access control across reviewer, compliance, ops, and admin roles
  • Tenant-aware data partitioning with case- and queue-level access checks
  • Environment separation: development, staging, production
  • Least-privilege service-to-service permissions; admin actions logged
4
Workflow Orchestration and State Management
Coordinates progression across ingestion, extraction, review, follow-up, escalation, and final decisioning — with predictable behavior under both normal and failure conditions.
  • Explicit state machine for the KYB case lifecycle
  • Event-triggered transitions with retry-safe progression across async steps
  • Exception and dead-letter handling; manual override with audit trail
  • Re-entry into workflow after customer response or reprocessing
5
Processing Job Orchestration
Background job execution for ingestion, OCR, extraction, chunking, retrieval preparation, and reprocessing — observable, retryable, and case-linked.
  • Asynchronous job execution with queueing and prioritisation
  • Idempotent retry handling and partial failure detection
  • Job state tracking with visibility into stuck or failed jobs
  • Operational observability across the full processing pipeline
6
Audit and Decision Logging Foundation
A foundational audit model built in Phase 1, not deferred. Every meaningful system and human action must be reconstructable for compliance, operational review, and dispute resolution.
  • Document uploads, extraction runs, model versions, and confidence flags
  • Reviewer edits, follow-up requests, escalations, and decision outcomes
  • Policy version used at decision time
  • User identity and timestamp for every material action
7
Policy and Retrieval Readiness Layer
Structured, versioned storage and retrieval of policy content so AI recommendations are grounded in the right rule set from the start — preventing ungrounded model behavior.
  • Policy repository with version control and metadata tagging
  • Tagging by geography, business type, risk class, and document context
  • Retrieval-ready indexing and policy-to-case applicability logic
  • Preservation of policy version used in each recommendation
8
Observability, Reliability, and Operations Controls
Operational instrumentation so teams can monitor health, detect failures, and diagnose workflow bottlenecks — without engineering deep-dives for routine issues.
  • Processing latency, job failure, and retry visibility
  • Queue depth and workflow state volume tracking
  • Alerting for failed processing or broken integrations
  • Basic operational dashboards for platform support teams
9
Extensibility Standards
Platform designed with reusable patterns for future regulated review workflows — without over-engineering Phase 1 or painting the platform into a KYB-only corner.
  • Reusable workflow state model with modular service boundaries
  • Pluggable connectors and event-driven architecture where useful
  • Case-scoped but use-case-extensible data model
  • Version-safe processing contracts
Phase 1 Outcome
By the end of Phase 1, Reap has a production-grade operational backbone that can securely store artifacts, coordinate KYB workflows, support AI-assisted review, preserve complete audit history, and provide the reliability needed for live onboarding operations.
Phase 2 Communications & Learning 2 modules · medium detail
Notification & Communication Module Phase 2
Internal Notifications
  • Review-ready case assigned; follow-up response received
  • Escalated case awaiting action; SLA threshold nearing breach
  • Processing failure requiring intervention
External Communication
  • Templated outreach with case-linked message history
  • Structured follow-up requests with response capture into case record
  • Email support with future channel expansion
Traceability
  • All messages, requests, responses, and reminders recorded against case
Guardrail: Outbound communication is reviewer-controlled or policy-bound — not fully autonomous. Messages are editable before sending.
Outcome: Reduce turnaround delays from fragmented communication, improve follow-up closure rates, and preserve a complete audit trail of case-related interactions.
Feedback & Learning Loop Phase 2
Reviewer Feedback Capture
  • Structured marking: correct / partially correct / incorrect / not useful
  • Free-text feedback on extractions, recommendations, and follow-up drafts
Implicit Signals
  • Edit-based signals from reviewer field corrections and rationale edits
  • Outcome linkage: accepted without edits, frequently corrected fields, escalation patterns
  • False-positive / false-negative recommendation tracking
Feedback Governance
  • Feedback enters a governed review pipeline — no direct live system retraining
  • Controlled model or rules improvement via triage and analysis
  • Version comparison across model, prompt, extraction, and policy changes
Outcome: Create a measurable path from reviewer behavior to product improvement — without compromising compliance control or model governance.
