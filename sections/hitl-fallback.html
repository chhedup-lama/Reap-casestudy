Block 6 — Governance

HITL & Fallback Control Model

AI prepares. Humans decide. This page defines where human control points sit, when AI abstains, how overrides and escalations are structured, and what fallback paths guarantee compliance continuity if AI services degrade.

⚠
Design rule: No KYB case may be finalised without an explicit human reviewer action — regardless of AI confidence, automation level, or system state. The system supports decisions; it does not make them.

Human Control Points

Six named control points define where human authority is exercised. Each is non-delegable — the system cannot bypass or auto-complete any of them.

A

Case Assignment & Triage

A reviewer must be explicitly assigned before work begins. Cases may be auto-routed to queues, but no AI action proceeds without a named reviewer on record.

Trigger: Case enters Review Ready state
B

AI Output Validation

Reviewers confirm, edit, or reject every AI-generated output before it attaches to the case record. Summaries, checklist states, and recommendations are proposals until a human acts.

Trigger: Case enters In Review state
C

Override Decision

Any reviewer can override an AI output at any point. Overrides require a reason code and free-text annotation — not optional. Override data feeds the quality loop.

Trigger: Reviewer disagrees with AI suggestion
D

Final Decision

Approve, Reject, or Request Follow-up must be an explicit human action. No state machine transition may produce a final decision automatically.

Trigger: Reviewer completes review
E

Escalation Authorisation

Escalating a case to a Compliance, Risk, or Fraud specialist is a deliberate human action with a mandatory reason. Specialists must also explicitly record their decision.

Trigger: Complexity, policy trigger, or risk threshold
F

Re-review After Follow-up

When new information arrives after a Follow-up request, the reviewer re-opens the case. Delta analysis highlights what changed — but the reviewer must re-examine and re-decide.

Trigger: Awaiting Response → Re-review state transition

Low-Confidence & Abstention Logic

When the AI pipeline cannot produce a grounded, reliable output, it abstains rather than generates. Abstention is surfaced as a structured signal — not a silent failure.

When Abstention Triggers
  • Extraction confidence below threshold
  • No matching policy clause retrieved
  • Document parsing failure or OCR quality too low
  • Contradictory evidence across sources
  • Missing mandatory document type
What the System Does
  • Flags the specific field or section as low-confidence
  • Creates an open item for the reviewer
  • Surfaces the uncertainty clearly in the workspace
  • Does not generate a recommendation for that element
  • Logs the abstention reason in the audit trail
What the Reviewer Sees
  • Highlighted uncertainty indicator on affected fields
  • Open item with abstention reason and suggested action
  • Source document links for manual verification
  • Checklist item marked as requiring manual confirmation
🚫
No silent abstention. Every abstention event is logged as an audit event with the triggering reason. Abstention rates by document type and policy category are tracked as platform quality metrics.

Override & Escalation Design

Override Flow

Overriding an AI output is a structured action, not a workaround. The system treats every override as a quality signal.

1
Reviewer identifies disagreement

The reviewer marks a specific AI output — a field value, a checklist state, a recommendation, or a summary claim — as incorrect or incomplete.

2
Reason code selected

A structured reason code is required: e.g. Evidence Misinterpreted, Policy Not Applied, Missing Context, Document Parsing Error, Other.

3
Free-text annotation added

Mandatory narrative explaining the reviewer's reasoning. Becomes part of the immutable audit record alongside the original AI output and the corrected value.

4
Override logged and case continues

The override event fires an audit event. The reviewer continues with the corrected state. Override rate, reason distribution, and affected output types feed the quality dashboard.

Safe Automation Boundaries

The platform distinguishes between what automation may do unilaterally and what requires human confirmation. This boundary is fixed — it is not adjusted by confidence score.

ActionAutomated?Condition
Route case to review queue Allowed Always — no human needed
Pre-fill extracted fields in workspace Allowed Shown as proposals; reviewer confirms
Generate checklist from policy Allowed States are suggestions; reviewer confirms each
Draft follow-up communication Allowed Reviewer reviews and sends — not auto-sent
Generate case summary Allowed Reviewer validates before relying on it
Surface risk signals and flags Allowed Informational only — no auto-action
Send follow-up to applicant Blocked Reviewer must explicitly send
Approve or reject a KYB application Blocked Human decision always required — Phase 1–3
Mark a checklist item as complete Blocked Reviewer must confirm each item
Escalate a case Blocked Human must initiate with reason
Close or archive a case Blocked Post-decision human action required

Fallback & Audit Controls

Fallback Modes

If AI pipeline components become unavailable, the platform degrades gracefully — maintaining compliance process integrity at all times.

Normal Mode
  • Full AI assistance active
  • All outputs generated and presented
  • Reviewer confirms, edits, decides
Degraded Mode — Partial AI
  • One or more pipeline components unavailable
  • Affected outputs suppressed or flagged as unavailable
  • Remaining outputs still presented; reviewer informed
  • Open items created for unavailable extractions
Full Fallback Mode — AI Unavailable
  • All AI assistance suspended; all cases route to manual queue
  • Reviewer workspace remains fully functional as a structured review tool
  • Checklists, audit logging, decision recording, and escalation paths all intact
  • No degradation in compliance process — only in analyst throughput efficiency
  • Banner displayed to all reviewers; product team alerted automatically

Audit Event Triggers

Every action across the HITL lifecycle fires an immutable audit event. Key triggers:

AI output generated
