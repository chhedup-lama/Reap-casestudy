Block 1 — Executive Framing

Problem Statement & Strategic Context

Why KYB is the right first AI investment for Reap, and why solving it requires a platform — not a point solution.

Business Context

Company Model
FinTech Infrastructure
Stablecoin-powered treasury, cross-border payments, and spend management — making business finance faster and more efficient.
Go-to-Market
B2B + Infrastructure Layer
Direct B2B: cards, payments, and expense products. Infrastructure: embedded finance APIs for partners building on Reap's rails.
Positioning
Crypto as Rails, Not Identity
Stablecoin used as money movement infrastructure — extensible to normal fiat flows. Not a crypto-native product for crypto users.

Why KYB First

✓
Phase 1 Selection — Row 01
Onboarding / KYB / Account Setup Friction
Reap's ops team performs KYB to decide whether a business can be legally and safely onboarded. The current process is manual, document-heavy, and judgment-intensive — a direct bottleneck to revenue, compliance, and scale.
~40
documents / pages
per typical case
~40

across review, follow-ups, re-review
1st
lighthouse workflow
for the AI platform
Reason 01
High-friction & high-frequency
Repeated manual work at high volume — the ROI of AI assistance is immediate and measurable.
Reason 02
Document-heavy by nature
Aligns directly with document intelligence, extraction, and retrieval — Reap's core AI capabilities.
Reason 03
Cross-functional proving ground
Sits at the intersection of Ops, Compliance, and Policy — a strong test for enterprise AI workflows.
Reason 04
Clear automation targets
Repeated manual steps: document review, policy lookup, follow-up drafting, re-review, rationale writing — all automatable with AI.
Reason 05
Measurable success metrics
Handling time, follow-up loops, reviewer adoption, and decision consistency are all trackable with existing ops data.
Reason 06
Natural HITL requirement
Final decisions always require explicit human review — making KYB a safe and realistic first AI deployment in a regulated setting.
Reason 07
Reusable platform components
KYB capabilities extend directly to EDD, sanctions/adverse media review, support ops, and payment operations workflows.
Reason 08
Painful enough, bounded enough
High enough pain to justify investment. Scoped clearly enough to ship in phases without over-engineering.

Why Platform, Not Point Solution

KYB is the lighthouse workflow — but the right build strategy is shared services first, domain workflows on top.

01
KYB pain = broader capability gapDocument ingestion, extraction, retrieval, workflow state, decision support, and auditability are all reusable.
02
Narrow solution = brittle + duplicatedKYB-only builds create fragile logic, duplicated infrastructure, and zero leverage for adjacent workflows.
03
Core capabilities are horizontal assetsOCR/parsing, policy retrieval, citations, case memory, review workflows, evals, and observability serve every workflow.
04
Platform reduces future rebuildsCommon services harden once and reuse many times — no re-engineering for EDD, sanctions, or support ops.
05
Consistent AI quality standardsA platform approach enforces consistent standards for safety, traceability, release gating, and monitoring across all use cases.
06
KYB is the lighthouse, not the only workflowPlatform components built for KYB extend naturally to EDD, sanctions review, support ops, and payment operations.
07
Speeds expansion to adjacent use casesOnce core services are live, new workflow coverage is configuration and domain data — not re-architecture.
08
Build shared services firstRight mental model: shared services + domain-specific workflow configuration on top — not isolated point tools.
