Block 8 — Delivery Strategy

Platform Reuse Map

KYB is the first consumer, not the only one. Capabilities built for KYB should be abstracted into reusable services that later support KYC, merchant onboarding, and transaction monitoring — reducing duplicate build effort and compounding platform value.

Design Intent. Build core capabilities as shared, versioned platform services. Keep workflow-specific rules, UI logic, and operational configuration in the product layer. Multiple products reuse the same AI, policy, audit, and review infrastructure without coupling to KYB-specific logic.

Service API Contracts

Each platform service exposes a stable, versioned API. Consumer products integrate against the contract, not the implementation.

  • Versioned REST APIs as default; async patterns for long-running jobs
  • Structured request/response schemas with explicit error and fallback states
  • Idempotent retry behavior where relevant
  • Tenant-safe and auth-checked by design
  • Trace IDs for cross-service debugging
  • Backward compatibility for active consumers

Contract rule: KYB-specific business logic belongs in the product layer, not the service. Example — the Document Intelligence Service extracts and normalises; the KYB product decides which fields are mandatory for its verification flow.

Reuse Readiness Criteria

A KYB-built capability should only be considered platform-ready if it meets all of the following:

  • Generic interface — not KYB-coupled naming or structure
  • Tenant-safe and role-safe by design
  • Versioned contract with backward compatibility
  • Configurable rather than hardcoded rules
  • Observable and traceable independently
  • Testable without coupling to one product's test suite
  • Supports at least one plausible second consumer with limited rework

Risks if Reuse Is Not Designed Early

  • ✕KYB becomes a tightly coupled vertical — hard to abstract later
  • ✕Duplicate AI and compliance logic appears across teams
  • ✕Inconsistent audit and control behavior emerges by product
  • ✕KYC or transaction monitoring launches become slower and costlier
  • ✕Model and policy improvements cannot be propagated cleanly across products
Build shared intelligence once, apply it many times. KYB should prove the platform, not trap it.
