Detailed User Flows

These flows are defined at the logical workflow level, independent of whether the review experience is delivered through an embedded UI in an existing operations system or a dedicated review console. Each flow is specified as: actor + trigger + system behavior + state transition.

Primary Flows

Flow 01
Intake Source Platform / AI
Intake to Review-Ready
Trigger New KYB case submitted via form, upload, email, or internal system feed
Flow Steps
  • 1Intake source sends case data and documents into the platform
  • 2Platform creates or updates the case record; documents stored and linked
  • 3Document processing: OCR, classification, field extraction, completeness checks
  • 4Confidence scoring applied to all extracted fields
  • 5Retrieval service pulls relevant policy and knowledge context
  • 6Platform generates structured fields, open items, evidence links, and AI case summary
  • 7Case marked Review-ready and routed to relevant reviewer queue
States New → Ingestion complete → Extraction in progress → Review-ready
Output
Review-ready case record Structured extracted fields Missing items identified Relevant policy surfaced Reviewer queue assignment
PRD Requirements
  • No case marked review-ready until minimum processing is complete
  • Extraction or retrieval failures must be visible — no silent pass-through
  • Review readiness criteria must be explicitly defined and enforced
Flow 02
KYB Analyst
Review and Edit
Trigger Case enters analyst queue in Review-ready state
Flow Steps
  • 1Reviewer opens case — sees header, AI summary, extracted fields, evidence panel, open items, policy guidance
  • 2Reviewer inspects key fields against underlying source documents
  • 3Reviewer checks low-confidence, missing, or conflicting items
  • 4Reviewer edits extracted fields where required; adds notes or rationale
  • 5Reviewer resolves open items that can be closed internally
  • 6Reviewer determines next action: ready for decision, follow-up, or escalation
Output
Corrected and validated case record Reviewer notes captured Open items updated Next-step action determined
PRD Requirements
  • All reviewer edits must be versioned and auditable
  • AI-generated outputs must remain visually distinct from reviewer-entered inputs
  • Evidence inspection must be accessible without leaving the review surface
Flow 03
KYB Analyst
Follow-Up Drafting
Trigger Reviewer identifies missing documents, unclear data, or unresolved checks
Flow Steps
  • 1Reviewer identifies gaps; platform surfaces related open items and supporting evidence
  • 2Reviewer selects specific items requiring external clarification
  • 3Drafting panel generates or assists drafting of follow-up content
  • 4Reviewer edits and finalizes the follow-up request
  • 5Follow-up artifact logged against the case; case paused pending response
States Review-ready → Follow-up requested → Awaiting response
Output
Follow-up request created Unresolved items recorded Case paused pending response
PRD Requirements
  • Follow-up requests must be linked to specific missing items
  • AI-generated draft text must be distinguishable from reviewer-authored edits
  • Exact unresolved items that triggered the follow-up must be preserved
Flow 04
KYB Analyst Senior KYB Reviewer
Re-Review After Response
Trigger New documents or answers received for a case in Awaiting response state
Flow Steps
  • 1New response or document arrives; platform attaches it to the existing case
  • 2New documents processed through ingestion and extraction pipeline
  • 3Platform compares previous and new information — delta highlighted
  • 4Case moves to Re-review; reviewer sees prior open items, new evidence, changed fields, and captured notes
  • 5Reviewer validates whether the follow-up has been adequately addressed
  • 6Reviewer resolves case, issues another follow-up, or escalates
States Awaiting response → Re-review → Proceed
Output
Updated case with version history Delta comparison surfaced Re-review path advanced
PRD Requirements
  • Prior and new submissions must be clearly distinguishable
  • Resolved vs. unresolved follow-up items must remain visible
  • Re-review must preserve prior review context — no blind case reset
Flow 05
KYB Analyst Senior KYB Reviewer Compliance Reviewer Compliance Lead
Escalation Flow
Trigger Case cannot be resolved at analyst authority level — high-risk, ambiguous, or policy-sensitive
Flow Steps
  • 1Analyst determines the case exceeds their authority; selects escalation action
  • 2Analyst records escalation reason
  • 3Platform packages: extracted fields, evidence, policy context, open items, reviewer notes, action history
  • 4Case routed to escalation queue or named reviewer
  • 5Escalated reviewer reviews; may return with guidance, take over review, or make final decision
States In review → Escalated → Resolved
Output
Escalated case with full context Escalation rationale logged Ownership transferred or shared
PRD Requirements
  • Escalation must be structured and auditable
  • Full review context must move with the case
  • Escalation must not erase or overwrite prior reviewer input
Flow 06
KYB Analyst (if authorized) Senior KYB Reviewer Compliance Reviewer
Final Decisioning
Trigger All required checks complete or explicitly dispositioned; reviewer ready to conclude
Flow Steps
  • 1Reviewer confirms all checks are complete or explicitly dispositioned
  • 2Platform validates whether any blocking issues remain
  • 3Reviewer reviews final case summary, evidence, and notes
  • 4Reviewer selects outcome: Approve / Reject / Request follow-up / Escalate
  • 5Reviewer enters rationale if required by policy
  • 6Platform records: decision, actor, timestamp, evidence references, applicable policy version
  • 7Workflow moves to final closed state; downstream event emitted
States In review → Closed — Approved
Output
Final decision persisted Complete decision trail available Downstream event emitted
PRD Requirements
  • Final decision must always require an explicit human action in MVP
  • System must warn against decisioning with unresolved critical blockers
  • Final decisioning must be permission-controlled by reviewer role
Flow 07
Audit / Control User Compliance Reviewer Compliance Lead
Post-Hoc Audit / Review
Trigger Compliance review, QA evaluation, or dispute investigation on a completed case
Flow Steps
  • 1Audit or control user opens a completed or historical case
  • 2User reviews: case history, documents, extracted fields, reviewer edits, policy references, AI outputs, workflow transitions, final rationale
  • 3User reconstructs the timeline of events and basis for the final decision
  • 4User records audit observations where applicable
  • 5Case remains unchanged unless a formal re-open or exception process is invoked
Output
Full review trail available Decision auditability demonstrated Control issues identifiable
PRD Requirements
  • All historical artifacts must remain accessible and immutable
  • Policy version and evidence links used at decision time must be preserved
  • AI outputs and human overrides must both be visible in the history view

Supporting & Exception Flows

Critical operational flows that distinguish a production-grade system from a happy-path prototype. Required for completeness of the PRD.

08
Exception / Processing Failure
Platform Ops User
AI systems fail in non-business ways: OCR failure, ingestion error, retrieval timeout, broken document association.
  • 1Platform detects processing failure or incomplete technical execution
  • 2Case flagged with exception state — not silently promoted to review-ready
  • 3Reviewer or ops user sees exactly what failed and why
  • 4Case routed for retry, manual handling, or technical support path
  • 5Minimum processing requirements enforced before state advancement
Without this flow, the PRD assumes happy path only — a significant gap for production readiness.
09
Low-Confidence / Abstention
Platform KYB Analyst
Central to safe AI-assisted review — the system must know when not to recommend.
  • 1Platform detects weak extraction, weak grounding, or conflicting evidence
  • 2System refrains from making an overconfident recommendation
  • 3Affected items surfaced as low-confidence or unresolved — visually flagged
  • 4Reviewer explicitly required to inspect and decide manually
  • 5System logs abstention behavior for quality analysis
This distinguishes a trustworthy enterprise AI system from a demo — abstention is a feature, not a failure.
10
Reviewer Override
KYB Analyst
Reviewers may disagree with AI-generated summaries, extracted fields, or recommendations.
  • 1Reviewer inspects an AI-generated output or field value
  • 2Reviewer edits, rejects, or overrides the AI suggestion
  • 3Platform captures: original AI output, reviewer override value, actor, timestamp, optional rationale
  • 4Case state advances using the reviewer-confirmed value
Override handling is core to human-in-the-loop governance. Required for auditability.
11
Queue Assignment / Reassignment
Platform Ops Lead
Operationally, cases need to move between people and queues based on specialization, workload, and risk.
  • 1Case becomes review-ready or escalated
  • 2Platform assigns case to queue or reviewer based on routing rules
  • 3Reviewer or lead may reassign due to specialization, workload, or risk level
  • 4Assignment change logged and visible in case history
Often critical in real operations — queue management is a first-class operational concern.
12
Policy Update Impact
Platform Compliance
Policy may change while cases are in progress — in-flight cases need a defined behavior.
  • 1Policy source updated; platform versions the new policy
  • 2In-flight cases either continue under the original applicable version, or are flagged for re-evaluation
  • 3Reviewers can always see which policy version applies to a given case
  • 4Policy version snapshotted at decision time for permanent traceability
Especially critical in regulated environments — policy version must be preserved at decision time.
13
Bulk / Ops Monitoring
Ops Lead
Leads and managers need to manage queues at a population level, not just individual cases.
  • 1Ops lead views case queue dashboard
  • 2Platform surfaces counts by status, priority, aging, escalation, and pending follow-up
  • 3Lead reassigns, reprioritizes, or unblocks stuck cases
  • 4All operational actions are logged in the audit trail
Makes the PRD realistic for actual operations teams — queue health is a daily operational concern.
